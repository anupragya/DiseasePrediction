Our Story

Where data science meets clinical care

MediSense was built to close the gap between the power of predictive AI and the everyday need for accessible, early health insight.

Mission

Making preventive health intelligence available to everyone

The earliest intervention is always the most effective. Yet for most people, access to data-driven health analysis has been limited to expensive clinical settings — if available at all. MediSense changes that.

By applying machine learning to a rich dataset of patient health records, we surface patterns that would take a clinician hours to derive manually. We don't replace medical professionals; we give individuals and practitioners a faster, clearer starting point for the right conversations.

Our mission is simple: use the tools of data science to help people understand their health trajectory, so they can act before symptoms become conditions.

The Process

From raw data to a clear risk picture

A transparent three-stage pipeline that turns health metrics into actionable guidance.

01

Data Collection

Users input key biometric and lifestyle data: age, gender, BMI, blood pressure, glucose level, cholesterol, heart rate, smoking and alcohol status, physical activity level, and family history of disease.

02

Feature Analysis

Each metric is evaluated independently and in combination with others. The model weights factors according to their statistical significance in the training data, reflecting real-world clinical relationships.

03

Risk Prediction

The model outputs a risk score with a Low / Moderate / High classification, a factor-by-factor contribution breakdown, and a tailored recommendation for next steps — all displayed immediately.

Built on a robust, multi-feature dataset

Data Cleaning

All records were pre-processed to remove outliers, handle missing values, and normalise ranges prior to model training. The dataset provided is the post-cleaning version used directly in analysis.

Model Approach

A supervised binary classification model was trained using an 80/20 train-test split. Feature importance was calculated to understand each variable's contribution to the outcome prediction.

Validation

Model performance was assessed using accuracy, precision, recall, and AUC-ROC on the held-out test set. Results are regularly reviewed as more data becomes available.
